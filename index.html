MMCD: Multi-Modality Collaborative Decision-Making for Connected Autonomy with Knowledge Distillation

Rui Liu, Zahiruddin Mahammad, Amisha Bhaskar, Pratap Tokekar
University of Maryland, College Park

Overview of the MMCD framework with knowledge distillation. Each connected vehicle processes its own RGB data, LiDAR data, or both locally to generate feature embeddings, which are then shared among the connected vehicles. Upon receiving the shared data, the ego vehicle fuses the multi-vehicle, multi-modality features to generate its actions. During the training stage, LiDAR feature embeddings are used as a Teacher to guide the learning of RGB feature embeddings through cross-modality knowledge distillation.

Abstract

Autonomous driving has advanced significantly, but challenges persist in accident-prone environments where robust decision-making is crucial. A single vehicle's limited sensor range and obstructed views increase the likelihood of accidents. Multi-vehicle connected systems and multi-modality data approaches, leveraging RGB images and LiDAR point clouds, have emerged as promising solutions. However, existing methods often assume the availability of all data modalities and connected vehicles during both training and testing, which is impractical due to potential sensor failures or missing connected vehicles. To address these challenges, we introduce a novel framework MMCD (Multi-Modality Collaborative Decision-making) for connected autonomous driving. Our framework fuses multi-modal observations from ego and collaborative vehicles to enhance decision-making under challenging conditions. To ensure robust performance when certain data modalities are unavailable during testing, we propose an approach based on cross-modality knowledge distillation with a teacher-student model structure. The teacher model is trained with multiple data modalities, while the student model is designed to operate effectively with reduced modalities. Our approach achieves up to 20.7% improvement in driving safety compared to existing methods, demonstrating its effectiveness.

Motivation

A motivation scenario of multi-modality collaborative decision-making (MMCD) for connected autonomous driving. The purple dash line represents the exchange of vital information between vehicles, overcoming occlusions and sensor limitations enabled by MMCD. When the collaborator is missing or the LiDAR on the ego vehicle is not available, MMCD remains flexible and robust by utilizing available RGB data to maintain effective decision-making.
